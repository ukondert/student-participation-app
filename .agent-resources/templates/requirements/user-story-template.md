# User Story Template

This template provides a structured format for defining User Stories, incorporating the best practices of the **3 C's (Card, Conversation, Confirmation)** and the **INVEST** criteria.

---

## User Story (Card)

**Title:** `A short, descriptive title`

> **As a** `<role/persona>`, 
> **I want** `<goal/action>`, 
> **so that** `<benefit/value>`.

---

## Details (Conversation)

*This section is for capturing key discussion points, clarifications, and assumptions that emerge from the conversation about the story. It is not a full specification but a summary of the shared understanding.*

- **Clarification 1:** ...
- **Assumption 1:** ...
- **Technical Note:** ...

---

## Acceptance Criteria (Confirmation)

*This is the checklist that confirms the story is "done". Each criterion should be a testable statement.*

- [ ] **Given** `<a context>`, **when** `<an action is performed>`, **then** `<an observable outcome>`.
- [ ] **Given** `...`, **when** `...`, **then** `...`.
- [ ] Verify that `...`
- [ ] Check for `...` (e.g., error handling).

---

## INVEST Checklist

*Use this checklist to ensure the User Story is well-formed.*

- [ ] **I - Independent:** Can this story be developed and delivered on its own?
- [ ] **N - Negotiable:** Is the scope flexible and open for discussion?
- [ ] **V - Valuable:** Does this story deliver clear value to the end-user or business?
- [ ] **E - Estimable:** Is there enough information to estimate the effort required?
- [ ] **S - Small:** Is the story small enough to be completed within a single iteration/sprint?
- [ ] **T - Testable:** Are the acceptance criteria clear, concise, and testable?
